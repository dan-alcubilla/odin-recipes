Asado Negro Recipe

Imagen de freepik

Description

The Asado Negro is one of the most iconic and elegant dishes of Venezuelan cuisine, famous for its characteristic dark color and deep sweet-and-sour flavor. The star is the "muchacho redondo" (eye of round roast), which is seared at high heat in a pot with oil and sugar or papelón until the surface turns nearly black (without burning). It is then slow-cooked in a sauce made of red wine, onions, ají dulce, and spices, resulting in incredibly tender meat that melts in your mouth, smothered in a velvety sauce that is the pride of any family table.

Ingredients

  • The meat: One eye of round roast (about 1.5 to 2 kg).
  • For the marinade and sauce: Onions, garlic, bell pepper, ají dulce (sweet chili), red wine, Worcestershire sauce, salt, and black pepper.
  • For the caramelization: Grated papelón (raw cane sugar) or brown sugar and vegetable oil.
  • Additional liquids: Beef broth or water.

Steps

  1. Marinate the meat: A day before (or a few hours early), prick the meat and trim excess fat. Marinate with crushed garlic, salt, pepper, Worcestershire sauce, and some of the wine.
  2. The "blackening": In a large, heavy pot, heat the oil and add the papelón or sugar. When the sugar melts and begins to bubble with an intense dark color, place the meat in.
  3. Marinate the meat: A day before (or a few hours early), prick the meat and trim excess fat. Marinate with crushed garlic, salt, pepper, Worcestershire sauce, and some of the wine.
  4. Sear: Turn the meat to brown it evenly on all sides until it is practically black. This step is crucial for the final color.
  5. Add vegetables: Add the finely chopped onion, bell pepper, and ají dulce to the pot. Sauté for a few minutes until softened.
  6. Slow cook: Pour in the remaining marinade, the red wine, and some broth until the meat is halfway covered. Cover and cook over low heat for 2 to 3 hours, turning the meat occasionally.
  7. Reduce the sauce: Once the meat is very tender, remove it from the pot. Let the sauce continue to simmer until it thickens and becomes glossy.
  8. Slice and serve: Let the meat rest before slicing it into rounds. Serve them smothered in the hot sauce. It is traditionally accompanied by white rice and tajadas (fried plantains).
